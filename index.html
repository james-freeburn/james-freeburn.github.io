Introduction

The University of Queensland, I completed my degree with honours, embarking on a journey that sparked a profound curiosity for the universe. As a PhD candidate at Swinburne University of Technology, I study Gamma-ray Burst afterglows and fast transients, striving to understand the most energetic events in the universe.

A significant part of my research comprises work with the Deeper, Wider, Faster (DWF) Programme. DWF seeks to discover fast transients by employing simultaneous multi-wavelength observations and real-time data analysis. As a member of this innovative initiative, I am engaged in cutting-edge research, utilizing state-of-the-art observational techniques and data processing methodologies.

In the pursuit of understanding the cosmos, I have not only honed my data science and programming skills but also ventured into the exciting world of machine learning. Leveraging my expertise in Python, R, C, and C++, I have developed a machine-learning classifier to analyse complex astronomical data. This innovative approach has enabled me to extract valuable insights from extensive datasets, enhancing the efficiency and accuracy of my research endeavours.

In addition to my research, I actively engage in sharing knowledge and insights with my colleagues through presentations. These interactions not only foster a collaborative research community but also showcase my strong communication skills. The ability to effectively convey complex astronomical concepts to my peers reinforces my commitment to clear and impactful communication.

Moreover, my proficiency in data science and machine learning equips me with valuable skills that can be applied beyond academia. The potential for cross-disciplinary applications in various industries drives my passion for contributing to real-world problem-solving and innovation.
